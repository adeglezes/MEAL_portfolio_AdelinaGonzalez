# Data Ethics Guidelines

Ethical data practices ensure that information is collected, stored and used in ways that respect the rights and safety of participants and partners.

## Core Principles

### 1. Respect for Persons
- Voluntary participation.
- Informed consent.
- Right to withdraw.

### 2. Beneficence
- Maximise potential benefits.
- Minimise risks and burdens.

### 3. Justice
- Fair selection of participants.
- Equal access to participation.
- Balanced representation.

### 4. Transparency
- Clear purpose for data collection.
- Honesty about data use and limitations.

### 5. Confidentiality
- Data minimisation.
- Secure storage.
- Restricted access.

Ethical MEL practices reinforce trust and support safe, meaningful learning.
