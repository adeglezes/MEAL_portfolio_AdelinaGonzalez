# Generic Theory of Change (Simulated)

This Theory of Change provides a general structure that can apply to capacity-building, governance, resilience or knowledge-transfer programs.

---

## Impact
Long-term improvements in resilience, well-being, inclusion or system performance.

## Outcomes
- Increased adoption of improved practices or behaviors.  
- Strengthened capacities among participants, partners or institutions.  
- Improved quality of services or decision-making.  

## Outputs
- Training delivered to target groups.  
- Tools, resources or information products disseminated.  
- Partnerships and coordination spaces established.  

## Activities
- Capacity-building sessions and technical assistance.  
- Field visits and follow-up support.  
- Communication and knowledge-sharing activities.  

## Inputs
- Staff expertise, local partnerships, funding, technology and materials.  

---

## Assumptions
- Participants have the motivation and opportunity to apply new knowledge.  
- Partners remain committed throughout implementation.  
- External conditions (political, environmental, institutional) are sufficiently stable.  

## Risks
- Limited resources for follow-up.  
- Conflicting priorities among stakeholders.  
- Barriers to access (technology, time, safety, geography).  

---

This ToC can be adapted to specific program objectives and MEL needs.
